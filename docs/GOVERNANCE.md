# Bridgelet Governance

This document outlines how the Bridgelet project is governed, how decisions are made, and how contributors can participate in shaping its future.

Bridgelet is an open-source project built with transparency, inclusivity, and long-term sustainability in mind. Governance processes may evolve as the project grows.

---

## 1. Governance Structure

Bridgelet follows a maintainer-led governance model with open community participation.

### Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for:

- Reviewing and merging pull requests
- Managing releases
- Defining roadmap priorities
- Ensuring code quality and security
- Guiding the overall technical direction of the project

Maintainers act in the best interest of the project and its community.

---

## 2. Contributor Levels

Bridgelet recognizes different levels of contribution and responsibility.

### Contributor

Anyone who:

- Submits issues
- Proposes ideas
- Reports bugs
- Submits pull requests
- Improves documentation

All contributors are welcome and encouraged to participate.

---

### Committer

A contributor who has demonstrated consistent, high-quality contributions and may be granted direct commit access to the repository.

Committers are expected to:

- Follow project standards
- Review smaller contributions
- Uphold code quality and documentation practices

---

### Maintainer

A trusted contributor responsible for overseeing major decisions and repository management.

Maintainers:

- Approve or reject significant changes
- Resolve conflicts
- Coordinate releases
- Guide governance discussions

---

## 3. Becoming a Maintainer

Maintainers are selected based on:

- Consistent and meaningful contributions
- Technical expertise
- Demonstrated alignment with the project's vision
- Professional conduct and collaborative behavior

Promotion to maintainer status is decided by existing maintainers through discussion and consensus.

There is no fixed timeline for promotion; it is merit-based.

---

## 4. Decision-Making Process

### Proposals

Proposals for changes should be made through:

- GitHub Issues (for discussion)
- Pull Requests (for implementation)

For significant architectural or governance changes, contributors are encouraged to open an issue first to gather feedback before submitting code.

---

### Approval Process

- Minor changes may be approved by a maintainer.
- Major changes require discussion and general agreement among maintainers.
- Security-sensitive or architectural changes require careful review before merging.

Bridgelet primarily uses consensus-based decision-making rather than formal voting.

---

### Roadmap Prioritization

Roadmap priorities are determined based on:

- Project vision and long-term sustainability
- Community feedback
- Security considerations
- Ecosystem alignment (e.g., Stellar and Soroban updates)

Maintainers retain final discretion over roadmap direction.

---

## 5. Project Operations

### Release Management

Releases are coordinated by maintainers and may include:

- Bug fixes
- Performance improvements
- Documentation updates
- Feature additions

Release cadence may vary depending on project activity.

---

### Conflict Resolution

Bridgelet promotes respectful and constructive communication.

In the event of conflict:

1. Parties are encouraged to discuss concerns openly in issues or pull requests.
2. Maintainers may mediate discussions if necessary.
3. If consensus cannot be reached, maintainers will make a final decision in the best interest of the project.

Harassment, discrimination, or toxic behavior will not be tolerated.

---

### Communication Channels

Project communication primarily occurs through:

- GitHub Issues
- GitHub Pull Requests
- Repository Discussions (if enabled)

Additional community channels may be introduced as the project grows.

---

### Meeting Cadence

Bridgelet does not currently operate on a formal meeting schedule.

As the project scales, structured governance meetings or community calls may be introduced.

---

## 6. Transparency and Evolution

Governance processes are designed to evolve.

As Bridgelet grows:

- Roles may become more structured
- Voting mechanisms may be introduced
- Formal improvement proposals may be adopted

All governance changes will be documented and made publicly accessible.

---

## 7. Guiding Principles

Bridgelet governance is guided by:

- Transparency
- Security-first decision-making
- Inclusivity
- Long-term sustainability
- Respectful collaboration

The goal is to create an ecosystem that supports secure blockchain onboarding while remaining open and community-driven.
